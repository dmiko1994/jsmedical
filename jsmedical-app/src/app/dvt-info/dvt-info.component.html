DVT (Deep Vein Thrombosis)

DVT: An Overview What is DVT/PE?

Deep Vein Thombosis occurs when a blood clot forms in a deep vein. This type of blood clot typically develops in the lower leg, thigh, or pelvis. When a DVT breaks loose and travels to the lungs it is called a Pulmonary Embolism (PE).

A Silent Killer

  • Over 900,000 Americans are affected by a DVT and up to 100,000 DVT-related deaths occur in the U.S. each year
  • As many as 50% of the cases of DVT are "silent." Very often the first symptom is a fatal PE
  • DVTs are responsible for more deaths than breast cancer, prostate cancer, AIDS and car accidents combined
 DVT in the Healthcare Setting Cause and Risk Factors

Post-Surgical Blood Clots

  • DVT/PE issues occur as a result of hospitalization, surgery, and other healthcare treatment or procedures
  • 50% of blood clot occurrences are directly related to recent hospitalization/surgery, and most of them do not occur until after discharge
  • DVTs can occur up to 30 days post-discharge. Despite in-hospital prophylaxis in patients, the risk for DVT remains high for several weeks after hosptial discharge

Patient Risk Factors

In the absence of appropriate preventative treatment, patients will develop thrombosis in:

  • 10% to 40% of medical and general surgery
  • 40% to 60% of major orthopedic surgery

 The Solution Preventative Measures

Prevention is Key

  • More than 50% of patients who developed a DVT/PE did not receive appropriate preventative treatment
  • 70% of hospital/surgery patients who develop a DVT/PE after discharge are preventable

Mobile Pneumatic Compression: A Safer Alternative

  • When patients complied with proper wearing of the pneumatic compression device, the incidence of DVT dropped by as much as 72%
  • Mechanical prophylaxis is the preferred method of DVT prevention. It provides DVT reduction rates similar to those achieved by pharmacological prophylaxis without the risk of developing bleeding complications
  • Pharmacological prophylaxis could be completely avoided in most cases if proper mechanical prophylaxis protocols were incorporated
